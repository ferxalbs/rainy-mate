## 2024-05-30 - MessageBubble Re-renders and Referential Inequality
**Learning:** In highly dynamic components like streaming chat interfaces (`MessageBubble`), passing inline arrays (like `|| []`) as props to subcomponents immediately defeats the benefits of `React.memo()`. This happens because `[] !== []`, causing `useMemo` or `React.memo` to perceive the props as changed on every single render cycle. Furthermore, for `React.memo` to work on parent components, *all* child components receiving those props must also be memoized, otherwise a re-render of the parent cascades down unnecessarily.
**Action:** Always extract static fallback values (like `const EMPTY_ARRAY: never[] = []`) outside the component to guarantee stable references. Additionally, explicitly wrap helper subcomponents (like `ArtifactBadgeRow`, `PlanConfirmationCard`, etc.) with `React.memo()` if they are rendered frequently or within mapped lists.
